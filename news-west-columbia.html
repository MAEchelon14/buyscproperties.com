Market Spotlight · West Columbia

How West Columbia Became One of South Carolina's Best Investment Stories

From a working dry-cleaning corridor to a thriving River District, the story of State Street is also the story of a family that helped build it.

Carolina Real Estate & Investment Group Midlands Market Notes 8 min read

Some communities grow because people finally discover them. Others grow because families, business owners, and community leaders believed in them long before everyone else did.

West Columbia is both.

Today, West Columbia is one of the Midlands' most exciting areas for real estate, business, dining, and redevelopment. The River District has become a destination. State Street and Meeting Street are thriving corridors of activity. New businesses continue to open. Redevelopment projects continue to take shape. More people are discovering what longtime residents have known for generations.

But the story of West Columbia did not begin with recent growth. It began decades ago.

A Community Built on Belief

Jimmy's family has been part of West Columbia since the 1960s. Long before redevelopment plans, riverfront attractions, and new investment transformed the area, the Skipper family believed in the future of this community.

His father, H. Wayne Skipper, was an entrepreneur who built businesses, created jobs, and invested in real estate throughout the Midlands. Over the years, he owned multiple businesses, including Waper Janitorial Supplies and Chemicals, Southern Specialties, and a network of dry cleaning stores.

Wayne purchased properties along State Street and Meeting Street when many people overlooked those areas. He saw value where others saw aging buildings. He saw opportunity where others saw uncertainty.

His son, Skip Skipper, worked alongside his father and successfully helped operate Waper Janitorial Supplies for many years, continuing the family's commitment to local business and entrepreneurship.

Jimmy partnered with Wayne on numerous real estate investments and development projects throughout the years. Together, they bought, renovated, developed, and managed properties while watching West Columbia evolve one project at a time.

For the Skipper family, West Columbia was never simply a place to do business. It was a place worth investing in.

From Mill Town to Modern Destination

Like many Southern communities, West Columbia has experienced periods of growth, change, and reinvention. For much of its history, the city was tied to manufacturing, rail transportation, and the industries that helped fuel the growth of the Midlands. The Congaree River served as both an economic engine and a defining feature of the community.

Over time, city leaders, business owners, and investors began recognizing the tremendous potential of West Columbia's location, character, and riverfront assets. The result has been one of the most impressive redevelopment stories in South Carolina.

The River District has transformed former industrial and warehouse areas into a vibrant destination for dining, entertainment, shopping, and residential living. Public investment, infrastructure improvements, riverfront access, and private development have helped create an environment that attracts both residents and visitors.

State Street and Meeting Street have become symbols of that transformation. Historic buildings have found new life. Local businesses have flourished. New investment continues to strengthen the area's appeal while preserving much of the character that makes West Columbia unique.

That progress is not accidental. West Columbia's growth has come from a combination of private investment, long term local belief, and the support of a city that understands how important small businesses, property owners, and thoughtful development are to the future of a community.

Why West Columbia Is Growing Now

West Columbia is benefiting from something much larger than local redevelopment. South Carolina is experiencing unprecedented growth.

People from across the country are relocating to South Carolina in search of opportunity, affordability, lower taxes, a strong business climate, and a high quality of life. New residents continue to arrive from states such as New York, New Jersey, Illinois, Ohio, California, and Florida.

As South Carolina's population grows, communities that offer convenience, character, and value are attracting increased attention. West Columbia sits at the center of that opportunity.

Residents enjoy quick access to downtown Columbia, the University of South Carolina, Lexington County, Columbia Metropolitan Airport, major healthcare systems, and some of the region's largest employers. At the same time, the city has managed to maintain a strong sense of community and local identity. That combination is becoming increasingly rare.

The Strength of Location

Real estate has always been about location. That is one reason Wayne Skipper believed in West Columbia decades ago, and it is one reason so many people are choosing the area today.

West Columbia sits at the crossroads of commerce, education, healthcare, government, and transportation throughout the Midlands. Its proximity to downtown Columbia creates opportunities for both residents and businesses while still offering neighborhoods, local businesses, and community connections that feel distinctly different from larger urban markets.

For investors, entrepreneurs, homeowners, and developers, West Columbia continues to offer opportunities that are becoming harder to find elsewhere.

Looking Toward the Future

When Wayne Skipper began buying property along State Street and Meeting Street, few could have predicted what West Columbia would become. The warehouses, industrial buildings, and overlooked commercial corridors of decades past have gradually given way to thriving businesses, new developments, public gathering spaces, and renewed investment.

Before the headlines, before the new restaurants, before the redevelopment plans, there were local families, property owners, business owners, and city leaders who kept investing in the area because they believed in what it could become. The Skipper family was part of that story, and so was the City of West Columbia, whose business friendly approach and support for new businesses and responsible development have helped create the momentum we see today.

The story of West Columbia is ultimately a story about vision. It is about people who saw potential before it was obvious.

It is about businesses that chose to invest. It is about a community that continued moving forward. And it is about a city that continues to honor its history while embracing its future.

The future of West Columbia is bright, but the foundation was laid a long time ago by families, businesses, and a city that believed in what this community could become.
