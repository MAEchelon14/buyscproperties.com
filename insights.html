West Columbia Midlands market 6 minute read

Where History, Vision, and Growth Come Together

Some communities grow because people finally discover them. Others grow because families believed in them long before anyone else did. West Columbia is both.

Today, West Columbia is one of the Midlands' most active areas for real estate, business, dining, and redevelopment. The River District has become a destination. State Street and Meeting Street are thriving corridors. New businesses keep opening, redevelopment keeps taking shape, and more people keep discovering what longtime residents have known for generations.

But the story of West Columbia did not begin with recent growth. It began decades ago, with people who saw value where others saw aging buildings, and opportunity where others saw uncertainty.

A community built on belief

Jimmy's family has been part of West Columbia since the 1960s. Long before riverfront attractions and new investment transformed the area, the Skipper family believed in its future. His father, H. Wayne Skipper, was an entrepreneur who built businesses, created jobs, and invested in real estate throughout the Midlands, including Waper Janitorial Supplies and Chemicals, Southern Specialties, and a network of dry cleaning stores.

Wayne purchased properties along State Street and Meeting Street when many people overlooked those corridors. His son, Skip Skipper, worked alongside him and helped operate Waper Janitorial Supplies for years, continuing the family's commitment to local business. Jimmy partnered with Wayne on numerous real estate investments and development projects across the years. Together they bought, renovated, developed, and managed properties while watching West Columbia evolve one project at a time.

For the Skipper family, West Columbia was never simply a place to do business. It was a place worth investing in.

From mill town to modern destination

Like many Southern communities, West Columbia has moved through cycles of growth, change, and reinvention. For much of its history the city was tied to manufacturing, rail transportation, and the industries that fueled the Midlands. The Congaree River served as both an economic engine and a defining feature of the community.

Over time, city leaders, business owners, and investors began recognizing the potential of West Columbia's location, character, and riverfront. The result has been one of the most impressive redevelopment stories in South Carolina. The River District has turned former industrial and warehouse blocks into a destination for dining, entertainment, shopping, and residential living. Public investment, infrastructure, riverfront access, and private development have combined to create a place that draws both residents and visitors.

State Street and Meeting Street have become symbols of that transformation. Historic buildings have found new life, local businesses have flourished, and new investment continues to strengthen the area while preserving much of the character that makes West Columbia distinct. That progress was not accidental. It came from a mix of private investment, long term local belief, and a city that understands how important small businesses, property owners, and thoughtful development are to its future.

Why West Columbia is growing now

West Columbia is benefiting from something much larger than local redevelopment. South Carolina is experiencing remarkable growth. People from across the country are relocating here in search of opportunity, affordability, lower taxes, a strong business climate, and a high quality of life, arriving from states such as New York, New Jersey, Illinois, Ohio, California, and Florida.

As the state's population grows, communities that offer convenience, character, and value attract more attention, and West Columbia sits at the center of that opportunity. Residents enjoy quick access to downtown Columbia, the University of South Carolina, Lexington County, Columbia Metropolitan Airport, major healthcare systems, and some of the region's largest employers. At the same time, the city has held onto a strong sense of community and local identity, a combination that is becoming increasingly rare.

The strength of location

Real estate has always been about location. That is one reason Wayne Skipper believed in West Columbia decades ago, and one reason so many people are choosing it today. The city sits at the crossroads of commerce, education, healthcare, government, and transportation across the Midlands. Its proximity to downtown Columbia opens opportunities for residents and businesses, while its neighborhoods and local connections still feel distinct from larger urban markets. For investors, entrepreneurs, homeowners, and developers, West Columbia continues to offer opportunities that are harder to find elsewhere.

Looking toward the future

When Wayne Skipper began buying property along State Street and Meeting Street, few could have predicted what West Columbia would become. The warehouses, industrial buildings, and overlooked commercial corridors of decades past have gradually given way to thriving businesses, new development, public gathering spaces, and renewed investment.

Before the headlines, before the new restaurants, before the redevelopment plans, there were local families, property owners, business owners, and city leaders who kept investing in the area because they believed in what it could become. The Skipper family was part of that story, and so was the City of West Columbia, whose business friendly approach and support for responsible development helped create the momentum we see today.

The story of West Columbia is ultimately a story about vision. It is about people who saw potential before it was obvious, businesses that chose to invest, and a community that kept believing in itself.

That same belief still guides our work across South Carolina. We look for places with good bones and real potential, and we invest the way the Skipper family always has, with patience, local knowledge, and a long view.
